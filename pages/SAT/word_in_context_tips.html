SAT Word-in-Context Tips

Learn how to use clues, tone, and sentence structure to choose the most precise word on the Digital SAT.

SAT Reading and Writing · Craft and Structure

What Are Word-in-Context Questions?

Word-in-context questions ask you to select the most logical and precise word or phrase for a sentence. The correct answer fits the meaning of the passage exactly. It does not merely sound good.

These questions test reading comprehension as much as vocabulary. Your job is to understand what the sentence is saying, then choose the word that reinforces that meaning.

SAT-Style Example

In recommending Bao Phi's collection Song I Sing, a librarian noted that pieces by the spoken-word poet do not lose their ______ nature when printed: the language has the same pleasant musical quality on the page as it does when performed.

Which choice completes the text with the most logical and precise word?

  • A.jarring
  • B.scholarly
  • C.melodic
  • D.personal

The clue is "pleasant musical quality." The word that best matches that clue is melodic.

How to Approach These Questions

1. Summarize the sentence Put the sentence into simpler words before looking too closely at the answers.
2. Find the clue Look for a nearby synonym, restatement, colon, or transition that explains the blank.
3. Match the meaning Pick the word that supports the passage. Eliminate choices that add a new idea or change the tone.

Top Tips

Check the charge Decide whether the sentence feels positive, negative, or neutral. The answer should match that tone.
Reuse the text Search for words or phrases in the passage that say the same idea in a different way.
Pay attention to colons A colon often introduces an explanation. If the blank comes before a colon, the clue may come after it.
Lean on transitions Words like "however," "although," and "regardless" signal contrast. The answer may need to reverse the first idea.
Do not choose hard words just because An unfamiliar answer is not automatically correct. Use the words you know to eliminate bad choices first.
Look for roots If a word is unfamiliar, check whether you know a related form of the word.

Your Turn

The following text is from F. Scott Fitzgerald's 1925 novel The Great Gatsby.

Gatsby's restless movement was continually breaking through his punctilious manner.

As used in the text, what does "quality" most nearly mean?

  • A.Characteristic
  • B.Standard
  • C.Prestige
  • D.Accomplishment
