SSAT Math: Word Problems

Translate words into equations, diagrams, tables, and organized steps.

Core Knowledge

Identify the question: underline what the problem asks for.
Choose a method: equation, table, diagram, or backsolving.
Track units: minutes, dollars, miles, students, and pages cannot be mixed carelessly.
Check reasonableness: estimate before trusting your answer.

SSAT Strategy

Word problems reward structure. Translate one sentence at a time, write a simple equation, and check that your answer matches the story.

Practice Questions

  1. A book has 240 pages. Maya reads 30 pages per day. How many days will it take her to finish the book?
  2. Three notebooks and two pens cost $14. Each notebook costs $3. What is the cost of one pen?
  3. A train travels 180 miles in 3 hours. At the same speed, how far will it travel in 5 hours?
  4. Leo is twice as old as Sam. Their ages add to 36. How old is Leo?
  5. A school sells tickets for $8 each and earns $320. How many tickets were sold?
  6. A rectangle has length x + 4 and width x. If its perimeter is 32, what is x?
Show Answer Key

1. 8 days. 240 ÷ 30 = 8.

2. $2.50. Three notebooks cost $9, leaving $5 for two pens.

3. 300 miles. The speed is 60 miles per hour, so 5 hours gives 300 miles.

4. 24. Let Sam be x and Leo be 2x. Then 3x = 36, so x = 12 and Leo is 24.

5. 40 tickets. 320 ÷ 8 = 40.

6. x = 6. Perimeter is 2(x + 4) + 2x = 32, so 4x + 8 = 32.
