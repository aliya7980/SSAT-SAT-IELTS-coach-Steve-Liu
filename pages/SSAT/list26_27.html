SSAT Vocabulary Practice: CON & COV Words

List 26-27 practice for interpretation, agreement, hidden action, and desire words.

Vocabulary List 26-27

1. ConstrueverbTo interpret or understand in a particular way.
2. Consummateadjective / verbComplete or perfect; to bring something to completion.
3. ContentiousadjectiveLikely to cause argument; quarrelsome.
4. ContiguousadjectiveTouching or sharing a border; next to each other.
5. ContingentadjectiveDependent on something else; possible but not certain.
6. ContriteadjectiveDeeply sorry for wrongdoing.
7. ContrivedadjectiveArtificial, forced, or not natural.
8. ConventionaladjectiveTraditional, ordinary, or following accepted customs.
9. ConvivialadjectiveFriendly, lively, and enjoyable.
10. CopiousadjectiveAbundant; present in large amounts.
11. CorollarynounA natural result or consequence.
12. CorroborateverbTo confirm or support with evidence.
13. CosmopolitanadjectiveWorldly, sophisticated, or familiar with many cultures.
14. Countenancenoun / verbA facial expression; to approve or tolerate.
15. CoupnounA sudden, successful action, often a seizure of power.
16. CovenantnounA formal agreement or promise.
17. CovertadjectiveHidden, secret, or disguised.
18. CovetverbTo strongly desire something that belongs to someone else.

Exercise 3: Paragraph Using All Words

Read the paragraph and notice how each vocabulary word is used in context.

At first, the committee did not know how to construe the mayor's brief announcement, but the plan became clear after a consummate negotiator explained it. The issue was contentious because two contiguous neighborhoods wanted the same park, and funding was contingent on their cooperation. After making a rude accusation, one council member looked contrite, while another offered a contrived excuse that convinced no one. Instead of following a conventional meeting format, the chair created a convivial discussion with copious maps, surveys, and student letters. A natural corollary of the evidence was that both areas needed access, and several residents came forward to corroborate that claim. The city's cosmopolitan mayor would not countenance secret deals, even after a failed political coup by rival leaders. In the end, both sides signed a covenant to share the park, ending the covert rivalry that had begun when each neighborhood started to covet the other's resources.
