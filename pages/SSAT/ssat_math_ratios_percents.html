SSAT Math: Ratios & Percents

Proportions, unit rates, percent change, discounts, and mixtures.

Core Knowledge

Ratio: compares quantities, such as 3:5.
Proportion: two equal ratios. Cross-multiply when helpful.
Percent: means "out of 100." Convert 35% to 0.35 before multiplying.
Percent change: change ÷ original × 100%.

SSAT Strategy

For ratio problems, label every part. For percent problems, ask: "Percent of what original amount?" Most errors come from using the wrong base.

Practice Questions

  1. The ratio of boys to girls in a club is 3:5. If there are 40 students, how many are girls?
  2. A sweater costs $60 and is discounted by 25%. What is the sale price?
  3. 12 pencils cost $3.60. At the same rate, how much do 20 pencils cost?
  4. A score increases from 72 to 90. What is the percent increase?
  5. A recipe uses 2 cups of flour for every 3 cups of oats. How many cups of flour are needed for 12 cups of oats?
  6. 40% of a number is 18. What is the number?
Show Answer Key

1. 25 girls. There are 8 total parts, so each part is 5 students. Girls = 5 parts = 25.

2. $45. A 25% discount is $15, so $60 - $15 = $45.

3. $6. Each pencil costs $0.30, and 20 × $0.30 = $6.

4. 25%. The increase is 18, and 18 ÷ 72 = 0.25.

5. 8 cups. 12 cups of oats is 4 groups of 3, so flour is 4 × 2 = 8.

6. 45. If 0.40x = 18, then x = 45.
