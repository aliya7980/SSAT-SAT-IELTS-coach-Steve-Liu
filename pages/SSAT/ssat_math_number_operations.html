SSAT Math: Number Operations

Fractions, decimals, integers, order of operations, factors, and multiples.

Core Knowledge

Order of operations: parentheses, exponents, multiplication/division, addition/subtraction.
Fractions: common denominators make addition and subtraction possible.
Decimals: line up place values before adding or subtracting.
Factors and multiples: use prime factorization for GCF and LCM.

SSAT Strategy

On SSAT math, calculation is usually part of a reasoning problem. Estimate first, simplify fractions early, and avoid long arithmetic when answer choices let you eliminate.

Practice Questions

  1. Evaluate: 18 - 3(4 + 2) ÷ 2.
  2. What is 5/6 - 1/4?
  3. A number is divisible by both 6 and 8. What is the least possible positive value of the number?
  4. Which is greater: 0.72 or 7/10?
  5. The prime factorization of 84 is 2 × 2 × 3 × 7. What is the greatest common factor of 84 and 126?
  6. If x = -4, what is x² - 3x?
Show Answer Key

1. 9. Parentheses first: 4 + 2 = 6, then 3 × 6 ÷ 2 = 9, so 18 - 9 = 9.

2. 7/12. Use denominator 12: 10/12 - 3/12 = 7/12.

3. 24. The least common multiple of 6 and 8 is 24.

4. 0.72. Since 7/10 = 0.70, 0.72 is greater.

5. 42. 126 = 2 × 3 × 3 × 7, so the shared factors are 2 × 3 × 7.

6. 28. (-4)² - 3(-4) = 16 + 12 = 28.
